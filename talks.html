IDEO Teachers Guild: Iterative and inclusive design

60-min Virtual Workshop

  • What: Facilitated a virtual workshop on strategies for iterative and inclusive design under uncertain conditions. Participants prototyped "two-sentence plans" to simply communicate what they're trying with their communities and ideated strategies for involving non-dominant voices.
  • Who: ~50 educators and school leaders from across the US preparing for the 2020-21 academic year during COVID
  • Context: Part of a weeklong summit hosted by IDEO's Teacher's Guild and School ReTool
  • For more: Slides and recording (password: #ez1qL^#)
  • When: July 2020, Zoom!

ALX: Rapid Prototyping

90-min Virtual Workshop

  • Challenge: ALX offers coding bootcamp style 6-month programs in business, data-science, and other domains. In response to the pandemic and a significant philanthropic investment, they initiated a six-week design sprint to prototype ideas for new programs and tech. The team had been stuck in ideation and early design research and I was brought in to help turn ideas into testable prototypes.
  • What: Facilitated a 90-min workshop where participants rapidly prototyped ideas into super low-fidelity prototypes of experiences and products.
  • Who: 12 person design team working from Lagos, Nairobi, Johannesburg, Kigali, and Mauritius.
  • For More: This Milanote Board includes slides, documentation from the workshop, and some of my design notes and debrief.
  • When: June 2020, Zoom

Connected Learning Summit (CLS)

Presented in three sessions during the 2019 CLS:

  1. A workshop on remixable learning design tools: I introduces principles from our work and invited participants to hack existing guides and explore how to support others in remixing their own work.
  1. Strategies for localizing and translating Scratch into 50+ languages (with Chris Garrity and Champika Fernando).
  1. A symposium on spreading creative learning around the world. I shared my work with South Africa's Ministry of Education and other organizations in South Africa to (a) introduce a digital skills for all curriculum nationally and (b) pilot aspects of that curriculum and what a creative form of teacher development might look like with a partner org in rural Kwa-Zulu Natal.

Creative Computing Workshop at the Play! Africa Conference

We were invited by the LEGO Foundation and the South African Ministry of Education to introduce conference participants to the Scratch programming language and to model what creative facilitation might look like. Our workshop was oversubscribed and included participants ranging from classroom teachers who work in low-resource contexts to members of South Africa's Ministry of Education and a range of nonprofits and education organizations from across southern Africa.

Creative Computing Teacher Professional Development in Rural South Africa

I co-facilitated a creative computing workshop introducing teachers to how they might use Scratch within the curriculum and resource constraints of low-resourced township public schools. The teachers (15 of them) came from rural and township public schools in Kwa-Zulu Natal, South Africa. I co-planned and facilitated the workshop with a grassroots teacher organization based out of Durban and Johannesburg South Africa.

The goal of the workshop was twofold: (a) empower the educators we worked with to use Scratch offline in ways that empower kids to work on extended, ambitious projects and (b) to pilot how the Ministry of Education might introduce computing in low-resourced settings as part of an ambitious nation-wide digital skills for all curriculum.

Computer Clubhouse Summit: Bringing a community organizing approach to designing a learning community.

I facilitated a workshop on bringing community organizing principles to designing and leading a computer clubhouse. This included lessons on coaching, on distributing leadership to youth, and on building community power.
