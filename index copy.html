I recently completed my graduate work at the MIT Media Lab where I worked on creative design tools for educators, explored future models for learning, and experimented with ways to bring project-based learning online in response to COVID-19. I also contributed to Scratch (a programming language and community used by 200M people in 2019), developed a tool to support school leaders in iterative and inclusive planning, and strategized with partners ranging from governments (e.g. South Africa's Ministry of Education) to innovative schools.

Before MIT, I helped launch a pan-African University network with sites in Mauritius, Rwanda, South Africa, and Kenya. We remixed ideas from Minerva, Ashesi, High Tech High, 42, and many others as we tried to create a new kind of university: hands on projects, research, and internships replaced lectures and problem sets. As part of my work, I built and scaled our faculty team, managed accreditation partnerships, and negotiated our ambitious vision with the operational challenges of launching a university from the ground-up.

You can explore my research at my MIT page.
