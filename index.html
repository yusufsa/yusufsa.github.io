I recently completed my graduate work at the MIT Media Lab where I developed creative design tools for educators, explored future models for learning, and experimented with ways to bring project-based learning online in response to COVID-19.

While at MIT, I also contributed to Scratch (a programming language and community used by 200M people in 2019), developed a tool to support school leaders in iterative and inclusive planning, and strategized with partners ranging from governments (e.g. South Africa's Ministry of Education) to innovative schools.

You can learn more about my research on my MIT page.

Before MIT, I helped launch a pan-African University network with sites in Mauritius, Rwanda, South Africa, and Kenya. We remixed ideas from Minerva, Ashesi, High Tech High, 42, and many others as we tried to create a new kind of university: hands on projects, research, and internships replaced lectures and problem sets. I built and scaled our faculty team, managed accreditation partnerships, and negotiated our ambitious vision with the challenges of launching a university from the ground-up.
