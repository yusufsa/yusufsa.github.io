Ahlan (hello!) 👋🏽

We live in a world where many people don’t see themselves as creators. I seek to change that by building better tools and communities to make and think with.

I recently completed my graduate work at MIT where I contributed to Scratch, developed creative tools for educators, and advised ambitious education initiatives. I also co-taught a class on creative learning technologies with Mitchel Resnick.

Learn more about my MIT research here.

Before MIT, I led development (from ideation to scale-up) of degree programs at a pan-African University  with campuses in Mauritius, Rwanda, and Kenya and over $80M USD in funding. We remixed ideas from places like Olin, Minerva, 42, Ashesi, and High Tech High to create a new kind of university: students learn through projects, research, and industry placements.

Outside of work, I mentor youth at a community makerspace and coach MIT startups. Rumor has it I make the best cup of Chai this side of the Atlantic.

Learn more in my resume or through my talks and workshops.
